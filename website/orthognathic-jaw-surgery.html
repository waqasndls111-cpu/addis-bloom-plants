Orthognathic Jaw Surgery

The purpose of orthognathic surgery is to reposition a misaligned jaw to ensure that teeth meet properly and the jaw functions effectively. In addition to its functional purposes, orthognathic surgery can also help improve a patient's appearance by correcting facial imbalances like a receding chin or protruding jaw.

What is Orthognathic Surgery?

Orthognathic surgery, also known as corrective jaw surgery, is a type of surgical procedure performed by oral and maxillofacial surgeons in conjunction with orthodontists to correct a wide range of skeletal and dental irregularities in the jaw and face. Jaws that are imbalanced or in the wrong position can lead to a number of problems that can affect chewing, swallowing, breathing, and overall facial appearance. Problems with jaw alignment can be caused by injury, birth defect, or different growth rates between the upper and lower jaws.

Staten Island Oral & Maxillofacial Surgery offers the most advanced treatment options to correct major and minor skeletal and dental deformities in the jaw. Proper detection and treatment of jaw deformities can help manage chronic jaw pain, biting and chewing difficulties, or any other jaw related problems. The goal of orthognathic surgery is to improve the patient's ability to chew, speak, and breathe while also enhancing facial aesthetics. This procedure is typically considered when other orthodontic treatments, such as braces, cannot fully address the underlying structural issues of the jaw.

Frequently Asked Questions

What Are The Symptoms Of Misaligned Jaws?

There are several symptoms that indicate you may have misaligned jaws. Possible symptoms include: Difficulty chewing, biting, or swallowing; difficulty breathing; speech problems; chronic jaw pain (TMJ pain); chronic dry mouth; open space in bite; inability to fully close mouth; facial imbalance such as a protruding jaw or receding chin; and sleep apnea.

Who is a Candidate for Orthognathic Surgery?

Orthognathic surgery may be recommended for conditions such as malocclusions, facial asymmetry, jaw growth abnormalities, and breathing difficulties. Malocclusions include improper alignment of the teeth and/or jaws that can lead to overbite, underbite, crossbite, open bite, or other dental misalignment. Facial asymmetry can result from significant imbalances in jaw size or position. Jaw growth abnormalities may involve the upper or lower jaw not developing properly. Some jaw discrepancies can also lead to obstructive sleep apnea or other breathing problems that may be improved through surgical realignment of the jaws.

How Is Orthognathic Surgery Performed?

The correction of jaw deformities often requires a united effort between an oral and maxillofacial surgeon and an orthodontist. Before the surgery, the patient wears braces to align the teeth properly. Once the teeth are in their ideal positions, the surgeon performs the jaw repositioning procedure. Corrective jaw surgery can include repositioning all or part of the upper jaw, lower jaw, or chin. The surgery is typically conducted inside the mouth so there are no visible facial scars. After surgery, the patient may need elastics or other orthodontic appliances for further fine tuning of the bite. The overall treatment process can take several months to complete. Staten Island Oral & Maxillofacial Surgery collaborates with the patient's orthodontist to evaluate the condition and determine the right treatment option.

Can I See How Beforehand What I Can Expect to Look Like After Jaw Surgery?

Staten Island Oral & Maxillofacial Surgery uses a sophisticated 3-D computer modeling system to show how corrective jaw surgery will proceed and what a patient can expect their face to look like once surgery is completed. The office uses imaging systems and computer software to simulate orthognathic surgery. CAT scans, patient photos, and medical models help diagnose and plan each individual surgery. Virtual surgical planning can simulate the surgery to obtain a general prediction of the surgical outcome. The oral and maxillofacial surgeons outline procedure details and expected results during consultation.
