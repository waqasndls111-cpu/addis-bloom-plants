Bone grafting to help rebuild the jaw foundation for implant treatment

When jawbone volume is reduced, bone grafting may be recommended to improve support for dental implants or to preserve the site after tooth loss. The goal is to create a more predictable foundation for long-term restoration.

Bone grafting may help when

  • The jaw has thinned after missing teeth.
  • A future implant needs more support.
  • The area needs better shape for restoration.
  • The treatment plan includes staged healing.

Why it matters

Implants need a stable base. Bone grafting helps address reduced volume so the final restoration can be planned more responsibly.

Common outcomes

A better shape, more support, and a clearer path toward future implant placement after healing.
